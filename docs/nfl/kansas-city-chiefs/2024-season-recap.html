Chiefs 2024: A Season Built on Close Wins

How Kansas City turned one-score games into a 15-2 record

Published

July 14, 2026

The 2024 Kansas City Chiefs didn’t blow teams off the field — they beat them late, over and over. Across 17 games, Kansas City went 15-2 (88.2% win rate), and the story of that record is written in the margins: 5 of their 15 wins came by three points or fewer.

Week-by-Week Scoring

Show Code 
ggplot(kc, aes(x = week)) +
  geom_line(aes(y = team_score), color = "#E31837", linewidth = 1) +
  geom_line(aes(y = opp_score), color = "#666666", linewidth = 1, linetype = "dashed") +
  geom_point(aes(y = team_score, color = result), size = 3) +
  scale_color_manual(values = c("Win" = "#E31837", "Loss" = "#1a1a1a"), name = "Result") +
  labs(
    title    = "Kansas City Chiefs — Points Scored by Week (2024)",
    subtitle = "Red = points scored | Gray dashed = points allowed",
    x = "Week", y = "Points"
  ) +
  theme_minimal(base_size = 13)

Nothing about that chart looks dominant. The Chiefs rarely pulled away — their average scoring margin for the season was just +3.5 points per game, thin for a team that finished 15-2.

Living on the Margin

Show Code 
ggplot(kc, aes(x = factor(week), y = margin, fill = close_game)) +
  geom_col() +
  geom_hline(yintercept = 0, color = "black", linewidth = 0.4) +
  scale_fill_manual(values = c("TRUE" = "#FFB81C", "FALSE" = "#E31837"),
                     labels = c("TRUE" = "Decided by ≤ 3", "FALSE" = "Other"),
                     name = NULL) +
  labs(
    title = "Margin of Victory / Defeat by Week",
    x = "Week", y = "Point Margin (Team − Opponent)"
  ) +
  theme_minimal(base_size = 13) +
  theme(legend.position = "top")

Five of Kansas City’s wins were decided by three points or fewer — a Week 2 escape against Cincinnati (26-25), a two-point win over Denver in Week 10, a three-point win over Carolina, and two-point wins over Las Vegas and the Chargers in consecutive weeks (13 and 14). That’s a full quarter of the season’s wins coming down to the final possession.

Kansas City played it close no matter where the game was — the road record held up nearly as well as the record at home, a sign of a team that didn’t lean on a friendly building to win football games.

The Best and Worst of It

The best result of the year came against PIT in Week 17, a 29-10 win (+19). The worst came in Week 18 against DEN, a 0-38 blowout loss — a Week 18 finale that looks far more like a team resting starters with the top seed already locked up than a real measuring stick.

Takeaway

Strip out that meaningless Week 18 finale and Kansas City finished the meaningful part of its schedule 15-1, and did it the hard way — five wins by a field goal or less, a road record nearly as good as its home record, and no game all season that looked like a laugher in their favor. Teams that win close games all year either have something real (a quarterback who doesn’t blink, a defense that gets one stop when it has to) or they’re due for regression. The 2024 Chiefs bet on the former, and for one more year, it held up.
